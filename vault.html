The Vault

A digital museum dedicated to the moments that stopped time, defied logic, and became immortalized in cricket history. Scroll to explore.

2022
The Shot of an Emperor

MCG Magic: Down the Ground

90,000 fans holding their breath. 28 needed off 8 balls. Haris Rauf steaming in. What followed was a shot that defied physics—a back-foot punch straight back over the bowler's head for six. Virat Kohli didn't just win a match against Pakistan; he bent reality to his will.

2011
A Nation Exhales

"Dhoni finishes off in style!"

A 28-year wait ended with one majestic swing of the bat. MS Dhoni launching Nuwan Kulasekara high into the Wankhede night sky remains the defining image of Indian cricket in the 21st century. The captain delivered when his country needed him the absolute most.

2007
Pure Carnage

36 Runs. 6 Balls. 1 King.

Andrew Flintoff made the worst mistake in cricket history: he made Yuvraj Singh angry. In the inaugural T20 World Cup, Stuart Broad faced the wrath of a man possessed. Six consecutive deliveries dispatched over the Kingsmead boundaries, changing the T20 format forever.

2016
The Ultimate Heist

"Carlos Brathwaite, Remember the Name!"

19 runs needed off the final over of a World Cup Final. Ben Stokes with the ball. Four balls later, the match was over. Carlos Brathwaite struck four consecutive, monstrous sixes into the Eden Gardens stands, cementing the West Indies as the undisputed kings of T20 cricket.

2023
The One-Legged Wonder

Maxwell's Miracle in Mumbai

Cramping, collapsing, and unable to move his feet, Australia was reeling at 91/7 against Afghanistan. What followed was superhuman. Glenn Maxwell smashed an unbeaten 201 using only his hands and raw willpower, dragging his team to the World Cup semi-finals in the greatest ODI innings ever played.

2019
The Ashes Miracle

Stokes' Headingley Heist

73 runs needed, 1 wicket remaining. As Jack Leach famously wiped his glasses, Ben Stokes etched his name into Ashes immortality. A reverse sweep for six, an agonizingly dropped catch, and a slashed four over point completed the most dramatic, nerve-shredding run-chase in Test cricket history.

1998
The Master Awakens

Sachin Silences Sharjah

As a literal sandstorm halted play in the desert, a figurative one was about to begin. Facing a legendary Australian bowling attack and a massive required run rate, Sachin Tendulkar unleashed absolute fury. His 143 single-handedly dragged India into the final. It was the night the 'Little Master' became a demigod.

1983
The Underdogs

Kapil's Devils Shock the World

Defending a paltry 183 against the mighty, invincible West Indies, hope was fading. Then, Kapil Dev ran backwards for miles to take a breathtaking catch to dismiss Viv Richards. That single moment shifted the paradigm of global cricket forever, birthing a billion-strong Indian cricket obsession.
